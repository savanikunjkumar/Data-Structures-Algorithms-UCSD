---

## 📚 Topics & Module Breakdown

### Week 1: Programming Challenges
* **Focus:** Setting up the environment, understanding time limits (usually 1.0s in C++), and implementing **Stress Testing** (generating random test cases to compare a brute-force naive solution against an optimized one).
* **Key Problems:** Maximum Pairwise Product.

### Week 2: Algorithmic Warm-up
* **Focus:** Moving from exponential O(2^N) naive solutions to fast polynomial O(N) or logarithmic O(log N) solutions.
* **Key Problems:** * Fibonacci Numbers (Huge Fibonacci modulo M)
  * Greatest Common Divisor (Euclidean Algorithm)
  * Least Common Multiple

### Week 3: Greedy Algorithms
* **Focus:** Making the locally optimal choice at each step to find a global optimum. Proving that a greedy strategy is safe.
* **Key Problems:** * Fractional Knapsack
  * Car Fueling 
  * Maximum Salary (Largest Number)
  * Collecting Signatures (Covering Segments by Points)

### Week 4: Divide and Conquer
* **Focus:** Breaking a problem into non-overlapping subproblems of the same type, solving them recursively, and combining the results.
* **Key Problems:**
  * Binary Search (with duplicates)
  * Majority Element
  * Sorting (Optimized Randomized QuickSort with 3-Way Partitioning)
  * Number of Inversions
  * Closest Points (O(N log N) geometric algorithm)

### Week 5: Dynamic Programming 1
* **Focus:** Overcoming the limitations of Greedy Algorithms by exploring all possibilities safely without redundant computations (memoization and tabulation).
* **Key Problems:**
  * Money Change Again
  * Primitive Calculator
  * Edit Distance (String comparison)
  * Longest Common Subsequence of Two/Three Sequences

### Week 6: Dynamic Programming 2
* **Focus:** Advanced 2D Dynamic Programming.
* **Key Problems:**
  * Maximum Amount of Gold (Discrete Knapsack without repetitions)
  * Partitioning Souvenirs (3-Partition problem)
  * Maximum Value of an Arithmetic Expression (Placing Parentheses)
